About Us

We met in grad school at Carnegie Mellon. The first time we talked was at Phipps Conservatory during visit weekend in March 2012. Over that summer, Dan tried to impress Tati by emailing her tales of riding a motorcycle through Europe. She must have thought he was way cooler than he is.

That fall, on orientation weekend, we had our first date. Dan somewhat ambiguously asked Tati to go with him to an improv show. Tati agreed, and then invited a couple of their friends along. (Luckily, those friends couldn't make it.) Dan ended the night with "Did we just go on a date? Because I'd like it if we did." Tati said yes. Three years later, she said yes again, this time when Dan proposed.

Now we're living in San Francisco, where Tati does User Experience Research at Facebook and Dan finishes his PhD in Human-Computer Interaction remotely.

Hotels

Finding a moderately priced hotel in Pittsburgh is maybe a little harder than it should be. We've worked out four options that are the easiest we can get, but if budget is important, you may have more luck just going on Priceline or Hotwire.

Option 1: Hotel Indigo in East Liberty

This hotel is run by the Intercontinental/Holiday Inn group; it's their "Boutique" hotel, which means it's got some kind of interesting decor. Also, it's big; we have 45 rooms here reserved for $170/night, and it's right next to the afterparty venue. We will have a shuttle from the Indigo to the Mattress Factory for the wedding and then coming back after.

Stay here if:

  • You want the simplest option.
  • You definitely want to go to the afterparty.
  • You want two double beds instead of one large bed; 15 of our rooms here have this setup.
  • You want to stay in a up-and-coming/rapidly-gentrifying neighborhood of Pittsburgh that Tati and I like.
Tell them you're in the Tasse Vlahovic Wedding group, and book by May 24, to get the discounted rate (though their regular rate is not much higher).

Option 2: The Priory Hotel on the North Side

This restored Victorian hotel (formerly a monastery) is a Pittsburgh one of a kind. They're about a mile from the Mattress Factory and have a shuttle that can take you there (or anywhere downtown). We have 15 rooms reserved here: 10 queen rooms for $145 and 5 king rooms for $180.

Stay here if:

  • You want transportation to be easy.
  • You're not a late-night partier.
  • You want easy access to downtown touristy things.
  • You like the Victorian style.
  • You don't mind booking early.
Tell them you're in the Tasse Vlahovic Wedding group, and book by May 10, to get the discounted rate.

Option 3: Other places on the North Side

The North Side actually has a handful of other good bed and breakfasts, including the Inn on the Mexican War Streets ($160-220), the Caribbean-themed Parador Inn ($160), and the (coming soon) Allegheny Inn. There are also a good bunch of AirBnBs (around $100).

Stay here if:

  • You like small, independent B&Bs or the Priory was full.
  • You want to walk to the Mattress Factory (Inn on the Mexican War Streets is right around the corner).
  • You want to stay near the venue (and downtown attractions) but still save a few bucks.

Option 4: Other places in East Liberty

Two good options here: AirBnBs ($35+) or the Ace Hotel ($240+).

Stay here if:

  • You are looking for the budget option (AirBnB) or the definitely not budget but way too cool option (Ace).
  • You want to get a couple friends together and split a 2br or bigger AirBnB.
  • You want to see the less-touristy more-neighborhoody side of Pittsburgh.
  • You want to be close to the afterparty and any friends staying at the Indigo.

What's this AirBnB?

You can stay in someone's spare bedroom, or in some cases rent their full apartment. It's usually a lot cheaper and more interesting than a hotel.

Transportation

Pittsburgh's public transportation is not amazing, so driving in or renting a car is not a terrible idea. The Indigo has valet parking for $24/day, or there are nearby pay lots for $1/hr or $14/day (free after 10pm and on Sunday). The Priory has free parking.

Otherwise, to get to the city from the airport, take the 28x bus downtown (if you're staying on the North Side) or to the end of the line (if you're staying in East Liberty), and then either use public buses (Google maps works fine) or Uber/Lyft from there.

Our bike share is great way to get around too! Or rent a bike for the day from Golden Triangle.
